Nikola Tesla

The man who invented 20th century

“The present is theirs; the future, for which I really worked, is mine.”― Nikola Tesla

Here's a time line of Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Born on July 10, 1856, to a Serbian family in Smiljan then part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire now in Croatia
  • 1875 - Enrolled to study engineering at Austrian Polytechnic in Graz, Austria
  • 1882 - Started working for Continental Edison in Paris. While working, identified the rotating magnetic induction field principle, which is used today in alternators
  • 1883 - Sent to Strasbourg, Paris to fix damaged lighting system installed at German Railway company. Repairs approved but Tesla never received the compensation
  • 1884 - Took a boat to USA
  • 1885 - Approached by inventors to develop improved arc lighting system, in exchange for financing to form Tesla Electric Light Company. After project completion, Tesla was forced out of company, which he later described as "the hardest blow" he'd ever received
  • 1886 - Left with nothing but some worthless certificates, Tesla had to work as a ditch digger
  • 1887 - Started working on alternated current system funded by Alfred S. Brown director of Western Union in exchange for fifty fifty patent split
  • 1890 - Developed first neon and fluorescent illumination. Began experimenting with x-rays and took some of the very first x-rays photos. He also illuminated a vacuum tube wirelessly by transferring energy through air
  • 1891 - Patented Tesla Coil which is used to produce high-voltage, low-current, high frequency alternating-current electricity. Tesla became citizen of the United States same year
  • 1893 - Joined with George Westinghouse, Tesla's alternating current powers Columbian exhibition
  • 1894 - Voted member of the Serbian Royal Academy of Belgrade
  • 1899 - Moved his research lab to Colorado Springs to have larger space
  • 1901 - Began construction on Wardenclyffe Tower to demonstrate how electrical energy could be transmitted without the need for power lines. The project initially backed by J.P.Morgan who later didn't see any financial gain as the electricity couldn't be metered without powerlines, withdrew backing and discouraged other investors as well. Left without funding, project was never completed
  • 1913 - Patented blade turbine
  • 1915 - Filed lawsuit against Guglielmo Marconi for radio transmission patent infringement
  • 1943 - died of heart failure at the age of 86
